America's School Shooting Crisis

By Fiona Wilton | November 17, 2024

The United States is the country with the most school shootings in the world by a large margin. The United States also has the highest gun ownership rate in the world. The availability of guns in the U.S. is a leading cause of death in young people. Although Missouri has only experienced one large-scale school shooting in St. Louis, the need for preventative security measures in schools is more important than ever. The Columbia School District invested in metal detectors for the high schools earlier this year to maximize security. Columbia Public Schools have spent hundreds of thousands of dollars to purchase and install these systems.

Columbia public schools implementing weapon detection systems

Battle High School, Rock Bridge High School, and Hickman High School use weapon detectors at some entrances to the schools. The district purchased 15 OPENGATE weapons detection systems in April of 2024 for $260,726. The system is touchless and automatically screens individuals who enter the building for potential threats and other prohibited items. If the systems detect any prohibited items, secondary searches are conducted. School supplies such as laptops and large notebooks with metal binding set off the devices and must be removed from bags before entry.

Source: ABC News
Hickman High School
Hickman High School tested a touchless weapon detection system in December that would alert administrators to the presence of a gun, knife, bomb or other weapon before anyone entered the building. The company Evolv used AI to recognize ordinary objects like cellphones or keys but would produce an alert when weapons are detected. However, to implement these systems in all of the Columbia high schools and middle schools would cost almost $1 million, and Evolv is now facing a lawsuit for allegedly misleading shareholders, according to KMOU. For these reasons, CPS opted for OPENGATE.

Rock Bridge High School
Following the installation of OPENGATE weapon detection systems at the beginning of the 2024-2025 school year, Rock Bridge High School students faced long lines to enter the school. All students made it to their classes by 9:30 a.m., 25 minutes after the school day started. Since the initial installation, the system has become more familiar to students and do not prevent students from making it to class on time.

School shootings in the U.S. are increasing

The number of school shootings in America tripled between 2020 and 2022. There has been an increase in school shootings since the Columbine High School shooting in 1999.

Source: K-12 School Shooting Database, Everytown Research

School shooting incidents by state since 2013

According to Everytown Research, gunfire on school grounds occurs most often at schools with a high proportion of students of color, disproportionately affecting Black students. The research documents every time a firearm discharged a live round inside or into a school building or on or onto a school campus or grounds.
